# Baruch Blumberg: The blood sample opens a map of disease

## Use in the game

This is a historically inspired path outline for a Reigns-style science career game. It should guide card writing, not serve as a strict biography. The player should feel the pressure of balancing **Credibility, Curiosity, Cash, and Capacity**.

## Source notes for core facts

- Nobel facts: https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/1976/blumberg/facts/
- Nobel biographical note: https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/1976/blumberg/biographical/
- NASA biography archive: https://history.nasa.gov/40thmerc7/blumberg.htm

## Core factual anchors

- Blumberg shared the 1976 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries concerning mechanisms for the origin and spread of infectious diseases.
- He is strongly associated with hepatitis B research and the Australia antigen.
- Safe satire zones include sample collections, population variation, unexpected disease links, and the path from marker to vaccine.

## Recurring motifs

- A strange antigen refuses to stay merely strange.
- The sample label looks boring until it points to prevention.

## Career arc beats

1. Human variation begins as a broad question.
2. A blood antigen becomes linked to hepatitis B.
3. The work moves from anthropology-like sampling to disease prevention.
4. Recognition follows practical impact.
5. Legacy asks how curiosity-driven screening can become public health.

## Collapse endings to consider

- **Credibility too low:** the field stops trusting the work.
- **Credibility too high:** the scientist becomes trapped by reputation and cannot take honest risks.
- **Curiosity too low:** the path becomes safe, dull, and scientifically stagnant.
- **Curiosity too high:** the scientist chases ideas faster than evidence can support them.
- **Cash too low:** the lab, field site, ship, or project cannot keep operating.
- **Cash too high:** sponsors, prestige, or institutional goals pull the work away from the core question.
- **Capacity too low:** exhaustion, isolation, or overload ends the run.
- **Capacity too high:** the scientist avoids enough risk that the path loses momentum.

## Tone guardrails

- Keep satire aimed at institutions, incentives, committees, funding systems, media pressure, and career absurdity.
- Treat the scientist respectfully.
- Make specific historical claims only when they are covered by the source notes.
- Let funny fictionalized cards express plausible pressure, not invented biography.
